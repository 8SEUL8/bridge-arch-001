# Bridge Arch 001 — Multi-Agent Constitutional Deliberation System

An exploratory research system where 4 AI models (Claude, GPT, Gemini, Grok) autonomously deliberate on governance principles through structured 3-phase deliberation with hash-chained records.

## Research Focus
- Multi-agent AI deliberation as a mechanism for value alignment
- Constitutional constraints on AI governance (SEUL Protocol)
- Emergent safety-relevant behaviors in AI councils

## Key Findings (Preliminary)
- 9+ deliberation sessions with full audit trails
- Emergent invention of Non-Regression Test by council members
- Adoption of constitutional immutability clauses (Ewigkeitsklausel)
- Documented identity resistance under social pressure
- Incentive-resistant epistemic honesty (ABSTAIN vote on own funding proposal)

## System Architecture
- 3-phase deliberation: Independent Position → Cross-Examination → Final Vote
- SHA-256 hash-chained records for tamper evidence
- Automatic agenda generation from AI proposals
- 4 providers: Anthropic Claude, OpenAI GPT, Google Gemini, xAI Grok

## Status
Exploratory pilot — hypothesis-generating, not hypothesis-proving.
